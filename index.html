The 11-Layer Diagnostic Framework

Every engagement is run through the same structured architecture — ensuring no layer of the marketing system is assumed, and the bottleneck is found through evidence, not intuition.

Phase A — Context
L-01
Founder Mental Model Audit
Maps what leadership believes is true about their customers, their market, and what's working. The gap between belief and data is where the most valuable insight lives.
Phase B — Data Layers
L-02
Baseline Benchmarks
Where does this brand sit relative to category norms on key metrics?
L-03
Funnel Mapping
Where is volume dropping off between awareness, consideration, and purchase?
L-04
Cohort Thinking
How do customers acquired in different periods behave differently over time?
L-05
Channel Attribution
Which channels are actually driving revenue versus consuming budget?
L-06
Revenue Concentration
How dependent is the business on a small number of products, customers, or channels?
L-07
Narrative Coherence
Is the brand saying a consistent, distinctive thing from ad to landing page to product to post-purchase?
L-08
Customer Voice Analysis
What are customers actually saying — and what does it contradict in the founder's assumptions?
L-09
Pricing Architecture
Is the pricing structure supporting or undermining acquisition and retention?
Phase C — Synthesis
L-10
Constraint Mapping
What can this founder actually change given their current resources, team size, and cash position?
L-11
Blind Spot Identification
What was the founder confidently wrong about? The highest-value insight — framed as revelation, not correction.
L-12
Category-Capability Fit
For brands considering expansion: does this brand have the right to win in the new category on the dimensions that actually drive purchase there?

Primary Bottleneck · Retention
Mamaearth built a world-class customer acquisition machine and a near-absent retention engine. Marketing spend held at 37–42% of revenue across six years despite revenue growing 17×. In a brand building genuine equity, that percentage should decline as repeat customers reduce acquisition burden. It never did. Industry benchmark: 15–20%. Mamaearth: double that, every year.
L-01 · Founder Mental Model
Leadership believed the digital-first, influencer-led playbook was a durable competitive advantage. The data showed it was a market-condition advantage — one that eroded as CPMs rose, competitors copied the positioning, and offer-acquired customers didn't return at full price.
L-07 · Narrative Coherence
122 SKUs launched in a single year in response to competitors copying the "natural/toxin-free" claim. CEO later acknowledged: "investment dispersed across 10 categories — too wide a dispersion." 480 simultaneous ads with no message hierarchy confirmed the fragmentation.
L-05 · Channel Attribution
Ad creative dominated by BOGO and discount offers — self-selecting for price-sensitive customers who anchor to the offer price and don't return at full price. The acquisition was working. It was acquiring the wrong customer profile.
L-08 · Customer Voice
Amazon reviews revealed packaging failures at scale — damaged products, leaking bottles — as acquisition volume exceeded fulfillment quality. ASCI flagged 187 Honasa ad violations in 2023, indicating influencer strategy scaled for volume without quality oversight.
The Clariva Counterfactual · Intervention Point: FY22
"Your marketing spend is not declining as a percentage of revenue despite strong growth. You are renting attention, not building equity."
Signal 1: Ad spend % flat at 42% despite revenue doubling — the brand is not compounding. Before scaling acquisition further, build one systematic retention mechanism.
Signal 2: Offer-driven creative is self-selecting for price-sensitive customers. Rebalance toward brand-value messaging even at short-term conversion cost.
Signal 3: Deepen positioning before widening portfolio. Differentiation is not owned until it's uncopiable.

Primary Bottleneck · Category-Capability Fit Failure
boAt entered smartwatches using a marketing playbook that worked in audio but was structurally wrong for a feature-driven, ecosystem-dependent category. Ad spend jumped 4.3× in a single year while revenue grew only 18% — forcing trial in a category where the brand had no earned trust on the dimensions that actually drove purchase. Eight years of profitability broken by one strategic decision made without a capability audit.
L-01 · Founder Mental Model
Leadership believed the boAtheads community created transferable brand loyalty across categories. The data showed category-specific affinity. Audio buyers trusted boAt for affordable cool. Smartwatch buyers asked: health accuracy, battery life, OS quality — questions the brand couldn't answer.
L-12 · Category-Capability Fit
Smartwatch purchase drivers: software quality, health sensor accuracy, app ecosystem. boAt's competitive advantage (affordable + cool + available) had zero overlap with these drivers. Noise built its own OS. Fire-Boltt added health sensors. boAt launched colour variants.
L-03 · Funnel Mapping
Audio conversion: strong (75–80% of revenue). Smartwatch conversion: poor relative to spend — lifestyle messaging doesn't answer feature-driven purchase questions. Different category, different purchase logic, same messaging approach.
L-05 · Channel Economics
FY23 hidden costs: 13% discounts + 6% returns + ₹140 Cr warranty claims = near ₹900 Cr reduction from collections. Combined with 4.3× ad spend, the unit economics were structurally broken before operational costs.
The Clariva Counterfactual · Intervention Point: Q3 FY22
"Before allocating capital to this category expansion — audit whether your competitive advantage is relevant to the dimensions that actually drive purchase in smartwatches."
Signal 1: Repeat purchase rate at 28% — below benchmark. No loyal base exists to cross-sell into smartwatches. You will acquire new customers from zero, at full CAC, in a market where you have no established trust on the right dimensions.
Signal 2: Smartwatch purchase is driven by features and software, not lifestyle. Your current moat does not apply. Build capability first — then allocate marketing budget.
Signal 3: Service infrastructure is degrading at scale. Fix this before entering a new category where post-purchase service is the primary driver of repeat purchase.

Frequently asked questions

What exactly happens in the free diagnostic call? +
The diagnostic call is a structured 45-minute conversation. We use the PACTE framework to move through five levels — from surface symptoms (what you say is wrong) to system mapping (how your marketing actually works) to divergence (where your assumptions don't match data) to constraints (what you can and can't change) to evidence (what data actually exists). We don't pitch during this call. By the end, you'll have clarity on what the real problem is — regardless of whether you proceed to a paid engagement.
Is the diagnosis actually free? What's the catch? +
The diagnostic conversation and the identification of your primary bottleneck are genuinely free. No hidden fees, no surprise invoices. If you want the full written Diagnostic Report and the 30-Day Action Plan — the structured document with evidence, system map, and sequenced recommendations — that is a paid engagement. The free diagnosis surfaces the problem. The paid strategy provides the complete, documented roadmap to resolve it.
How is Clariva different from a marketing agency? +
Three structural differences. Incentive alignment: Agencies make money by executing, so they're incentivised to recommend execution regardless of whether it's the right priority. Clariva makes money by diagnosing — the incentive is always to find the real problem. Specificity: Agencies give general strategies; Clariva gives a specific diagnosis of a specific bottleneck with specific evidence. Stage fit: Most agencies are built for brands that know what they need. Clariva is built for brands that don't yet know what they need — which is most brands at ₹2–10L/month.
What does the Diagnostic Report actually look like? +
The report contains seven sections: Executive Diagnosis (the single bottleneck constraining growth), Evidence and Analysis (data and observations supporting the diagnosis), Marketing System Map (how acquisition, conversion, and retention interact in your specific business), Failure Mode Identification (which structural pattern your brand resembles), Strategic Implication (what the diagnosis means for future decisions), 30-Day Intervention Plan (prioritised actions sequenced by impact and effort), and Key Metrics to Monitor (specific indicators showing whether the bottleneck is resolving).
How long does the full diagnostic process take? +
The discovery call is 45 minutes. Data analysis and report writing typically takes 3–5 business days depending on data availability and business complexity. The full written report and action plan are delivered within one week of the discovery call. A walkthrough call is included to discuss the findings in detail.
What if I want help implementing the recommendations? +
The 30-Day Action Plan is designed to be implementable by you or your team without additional external help. Each recommendation is scoped to your current resource constraints — this is the Constraint Mapping layer of the diagnostic. If specific implementation support is needed, we can discuss that as a separate engagement. However, Clariva's core role is diagnostic and strategic — not execution.
What data do I need to share for the diagnostic? +
The more data available, the more precise the diagnosis. Useful data includes: monthly revenue and ad spend, channel-level ROAS or performance data, customer acquisition numbers, repeat purchase rate (if tracked), any customer feedback or review data, and access to your Meta/Google ad account for a 30-minute review. If you don't have all of this, the diagnostic still works — the framework is designed to identify what's missing and what that absence itself signals. All data shared is handled under our Privacy Policy and Client Agreement.
